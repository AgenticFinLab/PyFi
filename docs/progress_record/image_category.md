### **I. Content Theme**

This dimension describes the core economic or financial subject matter of the image.

1.  **Macroeconomic Indicators**
    -   *Covers: Trends or comparisons of GDP, CPI/PPI, unemployment rate, PMI, consumer confidence, etc.*
2.  **Financial Markets & Products**
    -   *Covers: Stock indices, individual stock prices (candlestick charts, time-series charts), exchange rates, cryptocurrency prices.*
3.  **Commodities & Real Estate Markets**
    -   *Covers: Prices of crude oil, gold, copper, etc.; housing price indices, transaction volumes, new housing starts.*
4.  **Bonds & Fixed Income**
    -   *Covers: Government/corporate bond prices and yields, credit spreads, issuance volume, default rates.*
5.  **Monetary & Fiscal Policy**
    -   *Covers: Central bank balance sheets, money supply (M1/M2), reserve ratios, fiscal deficits, government debt-to-GDP ratios.*
6.  **International Trade & Capital Flows**
    -   *Covers: Trade balance (surplus/deficit), import/export volumes, international investment position, foreign exchange reserves.*
7.  **Corporate Finance & Valuation**
    -   *Covers: Income statement/balance sheet structures, trends in financial ratios (P/E, ROE, etc.), valuation models.*
8.  **Industry Analysis**
    -   *Covers: Market size, growth rate, market share, and competitive landscape comparisons across industries.*
9.  **Investment Theory & Portfolio Management**
    -   *Covers: Efficient frontier, Capital Allocation Line (CAL), asset allocation (stocks/bonds), Sharpe ratio.*
10. **Risk Models & Management**
    -   *Covers: Value at Risk (VaR) models, stress testing, loss distribution, "black swan" event illustrations, option Greeks.*
11. **Economic Cycles & Market Theories**
    -   *Covers: Investment clock (e.g., Merrill Lynch Clock), economic cycles (recovery-overheat-stagnation-recession), bubble models (Minsky Moment), market sentiment cycles.*
12. **Microeconomic Principles**
    -   *Covers: Supply-demand curves, market equilibrium, cost curves, monopoly/competition models.*
13. **Demographics & Socioeconomics**
    -   *Covers: Population pyramids, dependency ratios, migration, education levels, income distribution (Gini coefficient) related to long-term economic trends.*
14. **Financial Systems & Infrastructure**
    -   *Covers: Monetary policy transmission mechanisms, financial product/workflow diagrams, payment & settlement systems, fintech concepts (e.g., blockchain, DeFi).*
15. **Organization & Regulation**
    -   *Covers: Financial regulatory architecture, corporate governance structures, diagrams of international financial institutions (IMF, World Bank).*
16. **Geospatial Economic Data**
    -   *Covers: Global/national/regional GDP, per capita income, growth rates, or industry distribution on heat maps or annotated maps.*
17. **Financial History & Documentation**
    -   *Covers: Timelines of historical events (e.g., the Subprime Crisis), historical photos of central banks/exchanges, early financial instruments, biographical profiles.*

### **II. Chart Type / Format**

This dimension describes the data visualization format or style of the image.

1.  **Line Chart** - The most common format for displaying time-series trends.
2.  **Bar Chart / Column Chart** - For comparing data across categories.
3.  **Pie Chart / Donut Chart** - For displaying proportions or composition.
4.  **Scatter Plot / Bubble Chart** - For showing relationships and distributions between two or three variables.
5.  **Table** - Presents data directly in rows and columns.
6.  **Diagram / Schematic** - Uses boxes, arrows, and icons to represent processes, hierarchies, or abstract concepts.
7.  **Radar Chart** - For multi-dimensional performance comparison (e.g., comparing fund attributes).
8.  **Heatmap** - Uses color intensity to represent data values in a matrix or on a geographical map.
9.  **Candlestick Chart / OHLC Chart** - Specifically for displaying security price movements.
10. **Photograph** - A real photographic image.
11. **Infographic** - A composite format, combining multiple chart types, icons, and text to summarize a topic comprehensively.
